Things I learned building my MagicPhoenix Voron 2.4R2 CBT kit

Work in progress

* Use two-part epoxy for the TAP magnets...superglue will let loose and ruin your day.  Drifting z-offset or sudden changes of .6mm might indicate a problem with the magnets.  JB Kwik (the 5 minute version of JB weld) is fine.  Scuff and clean the magnets first.  
* Read the R8 TAP errata.  The official manual is several years old and doesn't incorporate information you need for the updated models.  
* For the Phaetus Dragon High flow hotend, there is an updated toolhead STL on printables:  https://www.printables.com/model/625623-phaetus-dragon-hf-printhead-mount-for-stealthburne
* For the MagicPhoenix CBT kit, Use Cable_Cover_EBB.stl and Main_Body_EBB.stl from BTT’s [EBB/EBB SB2240_2209 CAN](https://github.com/bigtreetech/EBB/tree/master/EBB%20SB2240_2209%20CAN/STL) repo for the clockwork 2 extruder.  These are a better fit for the toolhead board.  There have been some filename changes so the MPX wiki docs are out of date.  
* You probably need some active cooling on the TMC 2209s even during initial setup.  Prioritize printing the fan mount from the Voron /skirts folder so you don't have to balance a fan under the printer.  I would contest the "cosmetic" status of that part.
* These [rollback stands](https://www.printables.com/model/456448-service-rockers-voron-v2-vtv1) are fantastic for electronics access if you have a big printer like a 350.

# Bed Fans

  
* Bed fans on the M8P need jumpers just like all the other fans.  I installed the bed fans long after the rest of the fans and forgot about the jumper.  Spent some time trying to figure out why they didn't work.
* Related to bed fans, I ended up drilling a hole under the bed for the bed fan wires.  A regular twist drill will tear up the plastic, but a step drill works very nicely.  I was drilling from the bottom--you can use a thin piece of metal against the bed heater to prevent the bit from damaging the bed heater.  If you're the US, Harbor Freight is a good place for a [cheap step drill](https://www.harborfreight.com/14-in-to-34-in-3-titanium-step-drill-bit-9-steps-70721.html).

I drilled a half-inch hole in the location shown and used a grommet to protect the wires.  The out fans I just ran the wire between the extrusions that support the bed and the deck plate.  It is not a tight fit.  Then I just pulled everything reasonly tight and secured it with an adhesive mount and zip tie underneath.
