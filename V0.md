Things I learned building my V0 Formbot kit

# no-drop nuts

I mostly used these 12 and 15mm versions, and the door latch version:

https://github.com/VoronDesign/VoronUsers/tree/8e5067f4f6457da8a552983dd210ec48c40be2ca/printer_mods/zruncho/V0_No_Drop_Nuts/stl

But also a few of these 10mm ones:

https://www.printables.com/model/745599-10mm-no-drop-nut-for-1515-extrusions

I did not like the 10mm ones as much but they had their uses.  

Page number references are the official Voron design assembly manual.  

* Needed 116 15mm no-drop nuts.  That included some extras I preloaded for accessories.  Print 125 and you should be in pretty good shape.
* Print 15 12mm no-drop nuts.  You shouldn’t need more than 12 even if you’re being super conservative.  3 is the absolute minimum.
* cable chain mount screws are too close together even for the 12mm no-drop nuts.  But this one is easy—just start the nuts on the screws and slide the entire assembly into the end of the extrusion (pg. 58).
* Use a naked nut in the left rear corner where “Foot_Rear_Left_Inlet_x1.stl” attaches (this is the foot the power socket goes into).  The edge is too close to the vertical extrusion and the no-drop nut will interfere.  It's not the nut on the bottom (I got confused here).  I eyeballed it after and I am reasonably confident a 12mm no-drop nut would have worked here.
* You need TWO latch no-drop nuts.  Those go on the inside of the front extrusion before you install the B idler (page 85).  (Yes, the github says 'x2' in the filename but sometimes reading is hard)
* Use 12mm no drop-nuts at the front-most edge of the top extrusions holding the y-axis rails .  On pg 63, from the top view, these are the nuts closest to the front.  The other two nuts on each side can be 15mm.  The idler mounts are too close to the edge and a 15m will interfere(see pg. 85 and 86 to see the idlers that will mount there).  
* I eyeballed later and it looks like 15mm nut holders would have worked here, but if you want to be extra careful:      * I used a 12mm for the top no-drop nut on the both of the rear extrusions for the rear panel.  It looked like it would be close to the edge for the rear panel mount (Pg 228).  A
   * I also used 10mm (all I had left) for the top ones on the front extrusions for the side panels, and the rear extrusions for the side panels, for the same reasons (pg 229, 230).
   * Same thing for the B extrusion outside nuts on pg. 127--use a 12mm on the outside nuts.

# Random Observations

* M3 x 10 is the correct screw length for installing the rubber foot on the leg with the filament runout sensor (pg.102).  It will bottom out, until the later step where you install the bottom panel under the foot (pg.223).
* The BTT Pi clone uses a different hole pattern than the official raspberry pi.
* I have a whole shelf of greases for automotive and other stuff.  I refused to buy yet another speciality grease for the linear rails.  I used "Mobil 1 Synthetic Grease" and the printer has been great with it.  However, it is a thicker grease so when the instructions say the gantry should slide easily...it will not.  I am running 7k max_accel with no issue.

# Sensorless Homing

* The manual did not have much to say about the definition of "bumps too hard into the rail" when evaluating the minimum stallguard value.  I found the most accurate way to check was to put a finger right next to where the print head or gantry will hit (so the right side x gantry bracket or the A side lower drive on the back right of the printer) and it became very obvious when the print head started bumping harder.  Basically I barely felt it, and then as I lowered the value it became a noticeable thump when homing.
* Read the [Voron sensorless homing guide](https://docs.vorondesign.com/tuning/sensorless.html) thoroughly...I skimmed it and missed some important things, like that if stallguard is set too sensitive (which it is by default) the printer will appear to home backwards.
