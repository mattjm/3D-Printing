Things I learned building my V0 Formbot kit

# no-drop nuts

I used these:

https://github.com/VoronDesign/VoronUsers/tree/8e5067f4f6457da8a552983dd210ec48c40be2ca/printer_mods/zruncho/V0_No_Drop_Nuts/stl

There are others but these were the least annoying to print on my old ender3.  Page numbers references are the official Voron design assembly manual.  

* Needed 116 15mm no-drop nuts.  That included some extras I preloaded for accessories.  Print 125 and you should be in pretty good shape.
* Print 15 12mm no-drop nuts.  You shouldn’t need more than 12 even if you’re being super conservative.  3 is the absolute minimum.
* cable chain mount screws are too close together even for the 12mm no-drop nuts.  But this one is easy—just start the nuts on the screws and slide the entire assembly into the end of the extrusion (pg. 58).
* Use a naked nut in the left rear corner where “Foot_Rear_Left_Inlet_x1.stl” attaches (this is the foot the power socket goes into).  The edge is too close to the vertical extrusion and the no-drop nut will interfere.  It's not the nut on the bottom (I got confused here).  I eyeballed it after and I am reasonably confident a 12mm no-drop nut would have worked here.
* You need TWO latch no-drop nuts.  Those go on the inside of the front extrusion before you install the B idler (page 85).  (Yes, the github says 'x2' in the filename but sometimes reading is hard)
* Use 12mm no drop-nuts at the front-most edge of the top extrusions holding the y-axis rails .  On pg 63, from the top view, these are the nuts closest to the front.  The other two nuts on each side can be 15mm.  The idler mounts are too close to the edge and a 15m will interfere(see pg. 85 and 86 to see the idlers that will mount there).
